General Highlights of BAPS Mohotsav:



  1. Spiritual Discourses and Satsangs
    2. These gatherings feature enlightening talks by senior swamis and learned devotees, focusing on the teachings of Bhagwan Swaminarayan, Pramukh Swami Maharaj, and the lineage of gurus within the BAPS.

  2. Cultural Programs:
    3. Traditional dances, dramas, and musical performances that depict the life of Bhagwan Swaminarayan, the work of Pramukh Swami Maharaj, and stories from Hindu scriptures are a staple of these celebrations. These programs often involve participants of all ages, showcasing the talents within the BAPS community.

  3. Grand Annakut Offerings:
    4. A spectacular array of vegetarian dishes is offered to the deities, symbolizing devotion and thanksgiving. The Annakut (literally "mountain of food") is a visual and spiritual feast, reflecting the community's culinary diversity and creativity.

  4. Rituals and Poojas:
    5. Special rituals, including arti (a ceremonial offering of light) and pooja (acts of worship), are performed. These rituals are conducted with great reverence and are central to the spiritual observance of the festival.

  5. Prayers for World Peace:
    6. BAPS celebrations often include prayers for global well-being and peace, underscoring the organization's commitment to universal harmony and the welfare of all beings.

  6. Exhibitions and Public Engagements:
    7. Many BAPS celebrations are accompanied by exhibitions that educate attendees on various aspects of Hindu philosophy, the history of the BAPS, and the life and teachings of its gurus. These exhibitions often incorporate interactive elements designed to engage a diverse audience, including children.

  7. Community Service Initiatives:
    8. Aligning with BAPS's ethos of selfless service, celebrations frequently include initiatives such as blood donation drives, health camps, environmental conservation activities, and charitable distributions to those in need.

  8. Global Connectivity:
    9. For significant celebrations, BAPS often facilitates participation from across the world, including live broadcasts of events, allowing the global community to partake in the festivities, prayers, and rituals.

  9. Personal and Collective Spiritual Renewal:
    10. Mohotsavs provide a time for reflection, personal growth, and spiritual renewal for attendees. They are encouraged to commit themselves afresh to the principles of dharma (righteous living), bhakti (devotion), and seva (service).

  10. Messages and Blessings from Spiritual Leaders:
    11. Recorded or live messages from BAPS's spiritual leader, currently His Holiness Mahant Swami Maharaj, offer guidance, blessings, and inspiration to devotees. These messages often highlight the festival's spiritual significance and encourage followers to live in harmony with BAPS's teachings.


    Each Mohotsav is unique, reflecting the local culture, the specific occasion being celebrated, and the current themes or messages emphasized by the BAPS leadership. For the latest information on a specific "Highlight Mohotsav" or to explore more about BAPS's upcoming celebrations, visiting the official BAPS website or contacting a BAPS center would provide the most current details and insights.
