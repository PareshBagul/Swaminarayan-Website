THE CONCEPT

The concept of Swaminarayan refers primarily to the spiritual teachings, philosophy, and religious practices established by Bhagwan Swaminarayan, the founder of the Swaminarayan Sampraday.

Bhagwan Swaminarayan, born as Ghanshyam Pande in 1781 in Chhapaiya, Uttar Pradesh, India, later came to be known as Sahajanand Swami and Bhagwan Swaminarayan after his spiritual enlightenment. He founded the Swaminarayan Sampraday, also known as the Swaminarayan sect or Bhagwan Swaminarayan's followers, during the late 18th and early 19th centuries.

Central to the concept of Swaminarayan are the following key principles and teachings:

  1. Devotion (Bhakti): Swaminarayan emphasized the importance of devotion to God, known as Bhakti. Devotees are encouraged to cultivate a loving relationship with God through prayer, meditation, and service.

  2. Morality and Ethics: Swaminarayan emphasized the significance of leading a moral and righteous life. His teachings stress the importance of honesty, integrity, compassion, and self-discipline.

  3. Spiritual Growth: Swaminarayan's teachings aim at spiritual upliftment and liberation (moksha). He emphasized the importance of self-realization, detachment from material desires, and the pursuit of spiritual knowledge.

  4. Unity and Harmony: Swaminarayan advocated for unity among people of different castes, creeds, and backgrounds. His teachings promote social harmony, mutual respect, and cooperation.

  5. Guru Tradition: Swaminarayan emphasized the role of a spiritual guru (Satpurush) in guiding devotees on the spiritual path. He himself initiated several sadhus (monks) and appointed them as gurus to carry on his teachings.

  6. Service to Humanity: Swaminarayan stressed the importance of seva (selfless service) to humanity. His followers engage in various charitable activities, including providing food, education, and healthcare to those in need.

Overall, the concept of Swaminarayan revolves around leading a life based on devotion, morality, spiritual growth, and service to others, with the ultimate goal of attaining salvation and realizing one's true self. Swaminarayan's teachings continue to inspire millions of followers around the world who strive to live according to his principles.

Swaminarayan Storie

In the Puranas, the name Neelkanth is associated with Lord Shiva, who drank the world's poison to redeem it. In the process, the poison turned his throat blue; Neel meaning blue, kanth, the neck.
Ghanshyam's sojourns, through the forests of India, also attributed Him the name Neelkanth, for His pilgrimage was to redeem.
He first visited sacred Haridwar - 'the gateway to Hari', on the holy river Ganga. The sacred shrines of the Himalayas open up after Haridwar. From here He arrived in Sripur where He encountered the first of many enticements.
The head of the mandir urged Him to lodge inside the walled area, safe from wild animals. Neelkanth declined. He neither feared wild animals nor death. He then sat in deep meditation under a tree. At night, a lion hunting in the forest approached Him. The lion licked His feet, circumambulated Him and then sat there. The inmates of the ashram observed this extraordinary spectacle.

Baps Origin

BAPS, short for Bochasanwasi Akshar Purushottam Swaminarayan Sanstha, traces its origin to the teachings and spiritual lineage of Bhagwan Swaminarayan (1781–1830), the founder of the Swaminarayan Sampraday. The organization was formally established in the early 20th century under the guidance of Shastriji Maharaj, also known as Shastri Yagnapurushdas.

Highlight Mohotsav

Highlighting a specific "Mohotsav" (festival or celebration) of BAPS (Bochasanwasi Akshar Purushottam Swaminarayan Sanstha) without a particular year or event in mind can be broad due to the numerous and varied celebrations conducted by BAPS globally. However, one of the most significant and recurring events in the BAPS calendar is the celebration of the birth anniversary of Bhagwan Swaminarayan and Pramukh Swami Maharaj, often observed as Swaminarayan Jayanti and Pramukh Swami Maharaj Janma Jayanti.

Best Pravachan

In the context of BAPS (Bochasanwasi Akshar Purushottam Swaminarayan Sanstha), the "best" pravachan or spiritual discourse would largely depend on personal spiritual goals, interests, and the aspects of spiritual life one is looking to deepen. BAPS, under the guidance of its spiritual leaders, has offered countless enlightening discourses covering various aspects of spirituality, moral living, and devotion. These discourses often draw from the Vachanamrut, Swamini Vato, and the teachings of Bhagwan Swaminarayan, as well as the lives and teachings of gurus in the BAPS tradition, including Shastriji Maharaj, Yogiji Maharaj, Pramukh Swami Maharaj, and the current guru, Mahant Swami Maharaj.
